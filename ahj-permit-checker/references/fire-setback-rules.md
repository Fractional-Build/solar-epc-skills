# Fire Setback Rules — Solar PV on Buildings

Fire setback requirements are the most frequently updated permit requirement
in the solar industry and the most common source of rejections on resubmittals.
Local fire departments amend these rules independently of building departments,
and updates are rarely announced publicly.

This reference covers the standard frameworks. Always confirm with the
specific AHJ — what's here is a baseline, not a guarantee.

---

## The Two Frameworks

### IFC-Based Requirements (most commercial jurisdictions)

The International Fire Code (IFC) framework, adopted in most states:

**Rooftop setbacks (sloped roofs):**
- 18" from ridge (measured horizontally)
- 18" from hip and valley lines
- 18" from eave (some AHJs require more — up to 36")

**Pathways:**
- 3-foot clear pathway from eave to ridge on each roof plane
- 3-foot clear pathway along the ridge

**Exceptions:**
- Roofs with <2:12 pitch may have modified requirements
- Hip roofs may substitute a perimeter pathway in some jurisdictions

### California Fire Code / Title 19 (California jurisdictions)

California has its own framework that differs from IFC and is amended
by individual fire departments more aggressively than most states.

**Standard California requirements:**
- 3-foot pathways as above
- Some AHJs (Los Angeles City, LADWP territory) require 4-foot pathways
- Bay Area jurisdictions (SF, Oakland, Berkeley) have local amendments
  that frequently exceed state minimums
- Some rural counties defer to CAL FIRE rather than local fire departments

## High-Scrutiny Jurisdictions

These jurisdictions are known to apply stricter or recently updated
fire requirements. Flag for manual verification:

**California:**
- Los Angeles City / LADBS — requires 4-foot pathways on certain roof types,
  additional smoke ventilation clearances for flat roofs
- Los Angeles County (unincorporated) — separate requirements from LA City
- San Jose — fire marshal runs independent plan check from building dept
- Sacramento County — updated setback rules in 2023, many packages still
  use 2021 standards
- Fresno — local amendment requires 36" eave setback vs. standard 18"

**Southwest:**
- Phoenix AZ — extreme heat provision, requires ventilation gap documentation
- Las Vegas / Clark County NV — recently updated to require 3D rendering
  showing setbacks for commercial systems
- Tucson AZ — requires fire department pre-approval before building permit

**Northeast:**
- Massachusetts (most AHJs) — require 3-foot hip/valley setbacks even on
  systems that would qualify for exception elsewhere
- Connecticut — local fire marshal approval required separately

**Mid-Atlantic:**
- New Jersey (several municipalities) — require fire department walkthrough
  before permit issuance, not just plan approval
- Maryland (commercial) — some jurisdictions require sprinkler assessment
  for BESS co-located systems

## Flat Roof / Commercial Rooftop Specific Rules

Flat-roof commercial installations have different requirements:

**Typical requirements:**
- 6-foot perimeter setback from roof edge (varies — some AHJs require 4ft)
- Smoke vent / skylight clearances: minimum 4 feet from any smoke vent,
  skylight, or HVAC unit
- Access pathways: clear 4-foot corridors every 150 feet (check local code
  — some AHJs require corridors every 100 feet)
- Stairwell and roof access hatch clearances: minimum 4 feet

**Common failure pattern:**
Design based on satellite imagery that does not show current HVAC units,
skylights, or roof penetrations. Site survey must confirm actual locations.
Plan sets based on outdated satellite imagery are rejected regularly.

## BESS Fire Separation Requirements

Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) have rapidly evolving requirements.

**NFPA 855 (primary standard, adopted by most AHJs for commercial):**
- Maximum 50 kWh per fire compartment without suppression
- 3-foot separation between units
- Fire-rated walls / separation distances for larger installations
- Hydrogen detection for lead-acid and some lithium systems
- Listed BESS equipment required — verify UL 9540 listing

**California (HPWES / Title 24 for commercial):**
- Cal Fire has specific BESS siting requirements that exceed NFPA 855
  in some configurations
- Some jurisdictions require BESS to be physically separated from the
  main building by specific distances

## How to Document Setbacks in a Package

The fire setback section of the package should include:

1. Roof plan showing all array boundaries with dimensions
2. All setback distances labeled on the plan (not just "3' TYP")
3. Fire department access pathway shown and labeled
4. Any ridge, hip, valley clearances called out separately
5. Flat roof: perimeter setback AND internal corridor layout

**Common failure pattern:** Package shows "3' SETBACK TYP" without
labeling each setback line individually. Some inspectors require each
setback dimensioned separately even if they are all the same distance.
