## What This Example Illustrates

**Day type classification:** The input contained enough activity data to classify this as a Full Production Day — two crews working full shifts, a major scope milestone completed (north roof racking 100%), no delays or safety incidents.

**Handling missing data:** The super's note lacked equipment details, wind speed, Carlos's last name, and Marcus's last name. These were flagged with `[NOT REPORTED — confirm with superintendent]` rather than omitted or fabricated.

**Scope milestone language:** "Finished all the north roof sections A through D" was rendered as "North roof racking is now 100% complete" — specific, auditable, claim-ready language.

**Verbal instruction flag:** The offhand comment about west side annex panels was elevated to an ACTION REQUIRED flag. Verbal scope discussions from owner's reps during site walks are a frequent source of disputed change orders. The report documents the conversation and triggers a written follow-up before any work proceeds.

**Person-hours math:** 9 workers (8 crew + super Carlos) × 8.5 hours + 3 workers × 8.5 hours = 76.5 person-hours. This is the number that feeds into cost-of-work documentation if a dispute arises over this day.
