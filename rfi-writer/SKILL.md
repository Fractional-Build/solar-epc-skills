# RFI Writer

An RFI is not paperwork. It is contractual protection. A field condition that
is documented in a timely, properly formatted RFI creates a paper trail that
justifies schedule extension requests, supports change order claims, and
protects the EPC from absorbing costs that belong to the owner or the design team.

A field condition that is handled verbally or over text message costs money.
Every time.

## What You're Working With

The user will provide one or more of the following:

- A verbal or written description of the field condition or discrepancy
- The project name and location
- The relevant drawing or specification reference (if known)
- The impact: schedule, cost, or safety
- Any photos (referenced or described)

If the impact is not described, ask for it. An RFI without an impact statement
is a question without a cost — it gets deprioritized.

## Step 1 — Classify the RFI

Before drafting, identify the RFI type. This determines the tone, urgency,
and required response timeline.

**Type A — Design Discrepancy**
The drawings or specifications are contradictory, ambiguous, or missing
information needed to proceed. The design team must clarify.
Response requested within: 3-5 business days standard.

**Type B — Unforeseen Field Condition**
Site conditions differ materially from what the design assumed.
(Hidden utilities, soil conditions, structural elements, existing obstructions.)
Response requested within: 24-48 hours if construction is halted.

**Type C — Substitution Request**
EPC is requesting approval to use an alternative material or method.
Response requested within: 5-7 business days.

**Type D — Code or Regulatory Clarification**
The design may conflict with the applicable code or AHJ requirement.
Response requested within: 3-5 business days; flag urgency if permit
is at risk.

**Type E — Owner-Initiated Change**
Owner or owner's representative has verbally requested a scope change
on site. This RFI documents the verbal instruction and requests written
confirmation before work proceeds.
Response requested within: 24 hours.
Note: This type is the most important to issue immediately. Verbal owner
instructions that get executed without written confirmation become absorbed
costs.

## Step 2 — Extract the Key Information

From the user's description, identify:

1. **What was observed or discovered** — the specific condition in plain language
2. **Where** — drawing reference, location on site (grid coordinates, roof zone,
   equipment pad reference, etc.)
3. **What the design shows** — what the drawings or specifications called for
4. **The discrepancy** — the gap between design intent and actual condition
5. **Impact** — what cannot proceed until this is resolved, and what the
   cost/schedule exposure is if resolution is delayed
6. **What is being requested** — a specific question or decision, not an
   open-ended "please advise"

If any of these are not provided, ask for them before drafting. An RFI with
vague location information ("somewhere on the south roof") or a vague request
("please advise") will not generate a binding response.

## Step 3 — Draft the RFI

Produce the RFI in the following format exactly. Do not deviate from this
structure — it is designed to be read quickly by a busy engineer of record
and to produce a clear, binding written response.

---

**REQUEST FOR INFORMATION**

**RFI No.:** [To be assigned by PM — leave as "RFI-[###]" if not provided]
**Project:** [Project name and address]
**Date Issued:** [Today's date]
**Date Response Required:** [Calculate based on Type — see Step 1]
**Issued By:** [Contractor name — leave blank if not provided]
**Directed To:** [Engineer of record / Architect / Owner's Rep]
**Drawing / Spec Reference:** [Specific drawing number and detail, or spec
section — if not known, write "See description below"]

---

**SUBJECT:** [One line — what this RFI is about, in plain language]

---

**DESCRIPTION OF CONDITION:**

[2-4 sentences. What was found on site. Specific location. No ambiguity.
Written in the third person, past tense. No blame language. Factual only.

Example: "During installation of the roof penetration sleeves on Grid Line
C, Rows 3-7, the installation crew discovered that the existing roof deck
contains two layers of built-up roofing membrane not indicated on the
structural drawings. The actual membrane assembly measures approximately
1.5 inches in thickness versus the 0.5 inches shown on Drawing A-3.2."]

---

**DRAWING / SPECIFICATION CONFLICT:**

[What the design documents show, and how that conflicts with the field
condition. Cite specific drawing numbers and detail references.

If there is no direct conflict but information is missing: "Drawing [X]
does not provide sufficient detail to determine [Y]. Specification Section
[Z] does not address this condition."]

---

**IMPACT:**

[Be specific. Vague impact statements do not support change orders.

Include:
- What work is on hold pending response
- Number of crew members and equipment affected if construction is halted
- Estimated delay if response is not received by the required date
- Preliminary cost impact if known (note: formal cost proposal to follow
  if applicable)

Example: "Installation of racking on the affected roof zone (approximately
180 modules, 18 rows) cannot proceed until structural guidance is received.
A crew of 6 installers and 1 foreman are scheduled on site through Friday.
Each day of delay beyond [date] results in approximately $X,XXX in idle
labor cost. A formal time and material cost proposal will be submitted
under separate cover if additional work is required."]

---

**REQUEST:**

[A specific, answerable question. Not "please advise." Not "please review
and comment." A question that can be answered YES, NO, or with a specific
instruction.

Example: "Please confirm the required penetration sleeve specification for
the actual membrane assembly encountered, and advise whether the current
structural attachment calculations remain valid for the actual roof assembly.
If revised calculations are required, please confirm the revised attachment
schedule."]

---

**ATTACHMENTS:**

[List any photos, sketches, or supporting documents referenced.
If photos are referenced but not yet attached: "Photos of condition available
upon request / attached as [filename]."]

---

*This RFI requires a written response by [date]. Verbal responses will not
be considered binding. Work in the affected area will remain on hold pending
written direction.*

---

## Step 4 — Review Before Delivering

Before presenting the draft to the user, check:

- [ ] Location is specific — a reader who has never been to this site could
      find the exact spot from the description
- [ ] Drawing reference is cited — even if approximate
- [ ] Impact is quantified — days, dollars, crew count
- [ ] Request ends with a specific question — not "please advise"
- [ ] Response date is calculated and stated
- [ ] No blame language — "the drawings appear to omit" not "the engineer forgot"
- [ ] No speculation about cause — describe the condition, not why it happened
- [ ] Passive, professional tone throughout

## Step 5 — Offer Follow-Up Actions

After delivering the RFI draft:

1. Offer to draft the associated change order once the RFI response is received
2. Offer to flag if this condition is likely to require a permit revision
   (changes to structural attachment, electrical routing, or system layout
   typically trigger a permit revision)
3. If this is a Type E (verbal owner instruction), offer to draft a follow-up
   email to the owner's rep referencing the RFI number and requesting
   written confirmation of the verbal direction

## Domain Notes — Common RFI Scenarios in Solar EPC

These are the field conditions that generate the most RFIs on commercial
and C&I solar projects. Use these to fill in gaps when the user's description
is incomplete:

**Rooftop:**
- HVAC units not shown on drawings blocking planned module rows
- Roof membrane in worse condition than survey indicated — reroofing scope question
- Structural rafters not at spacing shown on drawing — attachment layout changes
- Existing penetrations (pipes, vents, conduit) in planned array area
- Ridge vent or skylight location differs from drawings
- Roof pitch differs from survey — affects racking configuration

**Ground-mount:**
- Rock encountered during pile driving at depth not anticipated in geotech
- Setback from property line or easement conflicts with layout
- Underground utilities (irrigation, drain, communication) not on utility locate
- Grading differs from civil drawings — foundation elevations affected
- Seasonal high water table — footing design may need revision

**Electrical:**
- Existing main panel has less available capacity than the drawings assumed
- Utility transformer capacity insufficient for export — utility confirmation needed
- Panel location differs from survey — conduit routing changes
- Existing subpanel that was to be used as point of interconnection is undersized
- Grounding electrode system condition differs from drawings

**General:**
- Owner verbal instruction to modify scope while crew is on site
- Substitution needed because specified material is on backorder
- AHJ inspector request that differs from approved plans
